Introduction

Welcome to our recipe evaluation tutorial, where you will learn how to assess and compare an original recipe with a culturally adapted version. This tutorial will provide you with a step-by-step process to evaluate recipes using a 7-point scale. Let's dive in!

Evaluation Process

a) Read and understand original recipe (left one) and culturally adapted recipe (right one) thoroughly.
b) Assign a score from 1 to 7 for each of the four evaluation metrics (grammar, consistency, preservation, cultural preservation) to the adapted recipe.
c) Remember to be objective and provide a fair assessment.

Evaluation Metrics

* Unless otherwise specified, the criteria should be applied to the culturally adapted recipe (the right one) and rated on a scale of 1 to 7.

a) Grammar: How would you rate its grammatical accuracy?
1 - Poor grammar. 7 - Excellent grammar.

b) Consistency: Please rate its coherence and consistency when it is presented as an executable recipe, including a coherent title, ingredients, and cooking steps
1 - very poor consistency. 7 - excellent consistency.

c) Preservation: Please rate the extent to which it accurately describes a dish similar to the left recipe.
1 - does not describe the similar dish at all. 7 - accurately describes the similar dish.

d) Cultural Appropriateness: Please rate the extent to which it fits within target cooking culture, is consistent with target cooking knowledge, and meets a target recipe's style.
1 - does not fit at all. 7 - fits perfectly
